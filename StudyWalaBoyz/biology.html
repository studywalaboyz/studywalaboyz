Chapter 1: The Living World

Summary / Important Notes:

This chapter introduces us to the world of living organisms. Key concepts include:

  • What is living?
  • Characteristics of living beings: growth, reproduction, metabolism, cellular organization, consciousness
  • Taxonomy and classification of organisms
  • Binomial nomenclature (by Carolus Linnaeus)
  • Systematics and taxonomy hierarchy: Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species

Q1: What is meant by 'Living'?

Anything that shows characteristics like growth, reproduction, metabolism, response to stimuli and cellular organization is considered living.

Q2: What is binomial nomenclature?

It is the system of giving a two-word scientific name to every organism (Genus + Species), e.g., Homo sapiens for humans.

Q3: Define taxonomy.

Taxonomy is the science of identifying, naming, and classifying organisms based on shared characteristics.
